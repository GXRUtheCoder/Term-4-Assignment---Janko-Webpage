Design reflection

This reflection explains the design choices made for the Janko Gear site and how the visual language supports the advertised products.

Design rationale

Goal: create a clean, accessible brochure-style site to advertise outdoor apparel and accessories. The layout uses clear navigation and visual hierarchy so visitors can learn about the brand, view products, and contact the company quickly.

Colour and contrast

I chose a high-contrast palette: dark blue/black text on a light background, and a saturated blue accent for calls-to-action. This ensures legibility for users with low vision and meets typical contrast guidelines.

Imagery

Hero and product images show the gear in use in natural environments. This helps visitors imagine real-world performance and builds trust.

Accessibility & semantics

Semantic elements such as <nav>, <main>, and <header> were used to help assistive tech. Form fields have labels and keyboard-friendly controls. Alt text was added to images.

Relationship to product

The simple, functional design reflects the product promise — practical, durable gear. The site avoids flashy ornamentation and focuses on clear product information and dependable visuals.

Key takeaways

  • Use clear navigation and hierarchy
  • Prioritise accessible colour contrast
  • Images should support product story
